4.
All these are examples of signals, and no textbook on evolutionary theory is complete without a lengthy discussion on them.1 Signals emitted by a “sender” have explicitly evolved to alter the behavior of the “receiver” and are used to communicate with and influence the behavior of prey, predators, mates, competitors, friends, and family. A signal can take myriad forms: touch, sound, color, motion, light, odor, or some combination of these. No organism could live without emitting and receiving signals, but a misdirected or misread signal can also lead to death. Although evolution has honed the signal emitting and receiving skills and instincts of animals and plants over millions of years, the race to deceive a predator or beat a competitor for food or a mate is never over.
这些都是信号的实例，任何进化论教科书若不深入探讨信号现象，都称不上完整。信号由"发送者"发出，其明确进化目的就是改变"接收者"的行为，被用于与猎物、天敌、配偶、竞争者、亲友沟通并施加影响。信号形式千变万化：触觉、声音、色彩、动作、光线、气味，或是这些元素的组合。生物若不能发射与接收信号便无法存活，但错误传递或误读信号也可能招致灭顶之灾。尽管进化历程已用数百万年锤炼出动植物的信号收发技能与本能，但无论是欺骗捕食者，还是为争夺食物与配偶战胜竞争者，这场进化竞赛永无休止。
5.
This book is about an integral part of the financial services industry—an industry that deserves its reputation for dishonesty and deceit. Few have described the sad state of our industry better than Fred Schwed in his hard-hitting, timeless classic Where Are the Customers’ Yachts? Those of you who suffered through the global financial crisis of 2008 have probably exhausted every cuss word for us finance folks. If you aren’t an evolutionary biologist, you probably believe that deception is the sole preserve of our cunning species. Not so. Let’s start with green frogs.
本书聚焦金融服务业的一个重要组成部分——这个行业素以欺诈失信闻名。弗雷德·施韦德在其鞭辟入时的经典著作《客户的游艇在哪里？》中，对我们行业的可悲现状做出了最犀利的刻画。经历过2008年全球金融危机的读者，恐怕早已用尽所有脏话来咒骂我们这些金融从业者。若非进化生物学家，你大概会认为欺骗是人类这种狡黠物种的专属技能。事实并非如此。 让我们从绿色青蛙说起。
6.
Green frogs, like most animals, have a short window for mating. During this crucial period, males need to defend their territories aggressively to maximize their reproductive success. Larger males advertise their size and dominance by making calls (croaks, if you prefer) at a lower frequency than smaller frogs. Thus, a male green frog who is evolutionarily programmed to avoid larger and stronger green frogs will not enter a territory filled with low-frequency croaks. His brain equates low-frequency croaks with unbeatable rivals.
绿蛙与多数动物一样，拥有短暂的求偶期。在这关键阶段，雄蛙必须强势守卫领地以最大化繁殖成功率。体型较大的雄蛙通过发出比小蛙更低频的鸣叫（或称之为呱鸣）来彰显体型优势与支配地位。因此，一只被进化机制设定为回避强壮大蛙的雄性绿蛙，绝不会踏入低频蛙鸣回荡的领地——它的大脑已将低频鸣叫与不可战胜的对手画上等号。
7.
What do you do if you are a small green frog? Live without a mate and die a virgin? No, sir! Some small green frogs have found a way to lower their signals’ pitch, thereby communicating that they are larger than they actually are.2 This ability enables them to preserve their territories by fooling the larger males. Some small green frogs hijack a low-frequency croak to send a dishonest signal, thereby gaining evolutionary advantage. This dishonest signal is easy to produce and imposes a very small cost on the signaler, the small green frog. If you think the green frogs are devious little dudes, you should be suitably impressed with the shenanigans of the fantastic fiddler crabs.
若你是一只小绿蛙该怎么办？孤独终老至死未经人事？才不！某些小绿蛙掌握了降低鸣声频率的诀窍，借此伪装出比实际体型更大的假象。这种能力让它们通过欺骗大型雄蛙来保卫领地。有些小绿蛙会盗用低频蛙鸣传递虚假信号，从而获得进化优势。这种欺诈性信号极易发出，且对发出者——小绿蛙而言几乎零成本。 倘若你认为这些小绿蛙已是狡黠之徒，那么招潮蟹的鬼把戏定会让你叹为观止。
8.
Fiddler crabs have gone one up on green frogs by dishonestly signaling their prowess to females and other males.3 Male fiddler crabs have a single large claw that serves the dual purpose of fighting other males and attracting females. Researchers have found that females differentially prefer males with larger claws, and these well-endowed males also successfully deter other male rivals. When a male loses his large claw in a fight, he usually grows a new claw that is lighter in weight, slenderer, and weaker. However, its size can still be impressive, and it can look as potent as the previous heavier and deadlier claw.
招潮蟹在虚张声势方面比绿蛙更胜一筹——它们会向雌蟹和其他雄蟹夸大自己的战斗力。雄招潮蟹长有单只巨螯，既能与其他雄性搏斗，又可吸引雌性。研究发现雌蟹尤其偏爱螯钳更大的雄蟹，而这些天赋异禀的雄蟹也能成功威慑竞争对手。当雄蟹在战斗中失去巨螯后，通常会再生出重量更轻、更纤细脆弱的新螯。但新生螯钳的尺寸依然可观，外观与先前沉重致命的巨螯同样威猛有力。
9.
These regenerated claws fool both sexes. The females do not differentiate between these impostors and the males with the heavier “real” claws. Even the males with the “real” claws don’t pick a fight with males with lighter and weaker but impressive-looking regenerated claws. Researchers have found that up to 44 percent of males in a fiddler crab population can have regenerated claws. And you thought it was only us finance folks whom you can’t trust! If you think dishonest signals are emitted solely by animals, think again. Plants are masters of deception. Since they can’t run or hide, many of their survival strategies involve deceiving other plants or animals. Some of the best examples—or worst, if you happen to be a wasp—are certain species of Australian orchids.4 Australia has about 1,400 species of orchids, and around 250 of these have adopted the same strategy of deceiving male wasps to enable pollination.
这些再生螯钳能骗过两性。雌蟹无法区分这些冒牌货与长着更沉重"真钳"的雄蟹。就连拥有"真钳"的雄蟹也不会与那些螯钳更轻更弱但外形唬人的再生钳雄蟹争斗。研究人员发现，招潮蟹群体中高达44%的雄性可能拥有再生螯钳。现在你该明白，可不只有我们金融从业者不可信吧！ 若你认为只有动物会释放虚假信号，那就大错特错了。植物才是欺骗大师。由于无法逃跑或躲藏，许多植物的生存策略都涉及欺骗其他动植物。某些澳大利亚兰花就是绝佳例证——当然，若你恰巧是只黄蜂，这堪称最糟案例。澳大利亚约有1400种兰花，其中约250种都采用相同策略：通过欺骗雄蜂来完成授粉。
10.
Here is how it works. When a female wasp, who lives most of her life underground laying eggs, is ready to mate, she emerges from the ground and emits a distinctive pheromone that attracts the males. Many males typically descend on a female and start copulating in a frenzy. Nothing out of the ordinary here. Okay, agreed, a bit disconcerting, but you know what I mean. Enter the orchid. Many orchid species have figured out a way to emit the same pheromone that a female wasp emits. What is the result? The male wasps end up copulating with orchid flowers, thereby brushing against pollen, which they carry to other flowers, where the unfortunate wasps “mate” again and leave the pollen behind! I don’t want to give the impression that the living world is replete only with hustlers and cheats. Unlike green frogs, fiddler crabs, and Australian orchids, male guppies and coral snakes lead a more upright life.
其运作原理如下：雌蜂一生多数时间在地下产卵，当它准备交配时，会钻出地面释放独特的信息素吸引雄蜂。通常会有大量雄蜂聚集到雌蜂周围，开始疯狂交配。这现象本不足为奇——好吧，确实有点令人不安，但你们明白我的意思。 这时兰花登场了。许多兰花品种能模拟雌蜂释放相同的信息素。结果如何？雄蜂最终与兰花发生交配行为，在摩擦间沾上花粉，又将花粉带到其他兰花上。这些倒霉的雄蜂会再次"求偶"，从而完成传粉任务！ 不过我不想让人误以为生物界尽是欺诈之徒。与绿蛙、招潮蟹和澳大利亚兰花不同，雄性孔雀鱼和珊瑚蛇就过着更为正直的生活。
11.
Guppies populate the mountain forest streams of Venezuela, Trinidad, and Tobago. Whereas female guppies are plain and drab-looking, male guppies are quite colorful, and no two males have the same color pattern. I urge you to do a Google image search of male guppies to see how dazzlingly beautiful they are.
孔雀鱼栖息于委内瑞拉、特立尼达和多巴哥的山林溪流中。雌性孔雀鱼外表朴素单调，雄性却色彩斑斓，且每只雄鱼的花纹都独一无二。我强烈建议你在谷歌图片搜索雄性孔雀鱼，它们绚丽夺目的模样定会让你惊叹不已。
12.
Scientists have experimentally demonstrated that females have a clear preference for brighter and more conspicuously red males with greater quantities of carotenoid pigments. More on carotenoids shortly. But conspicuous coloration comes at a huge cost to the males—their life.5 More colorful guppies are also subject to higher predation. If the males have dull colors, they won’t get to mate, but if they are brightly colored, they are likely to be hunted down. Talk about being between a rock and a hard place. Thus, brighter colors in a guppy are an honest signal of attractiveness to females. As Godin and McDonough succinctly state, “The viability cost associated with bright conspicuous coloration in male guppies potentially reinforces for females the reliability of this sexually selected trait as an indicator trait of male quality.”6
科学家通过实验证实，雌性明显更青睐体色鲜艳明亮、类胡萝卜素含量更高且红色更醒目的雄性。关于类胡萝卜素的详细讨论稍后进行。但鲜艳体色对雄性而言代价巨大——甚至要以生命为代价。体色越鲜艳的孔雀鱼遭受捕食的风险也越高。若雄性体色黯淡，它们将失去交配机会；但若体色过于鲜艳，又极易成为捕食者的目标。这可谓进退维谷。因此，孔雀鱼鲜艳的体色向雌性传递着真实的吸引力信号。正如戈丹和麦克唐纳的精辟总结："雄性孔雀鱼鲜艳醒目体色伴随的生存代价，反而强化了雌性对这种性选择特征的信任——它是衡量雄性品质的真实指标。"
13.
While a male guppy may attract predators by honestly communicating to females, the deadly coral snake deters its potential predators with its bright coloration.7 About ninety species of these fabulously colored and highly poisonous snakes are native to the tropics. They have alternating patterns of three colors: red, black, and yellow or white. This kind of aposematic (what a nice-sounding word from the world of biology!) or conspicuous coloration is quite common among many poisonous plants and animals. They are essentially broadcasting honestly to their potential predators, “Don’t you dare!” But what would evolution be if not bizarre and fascinating? Nonvenomous milk snakes kept as pets have almost the same coloration and banding pattern of coral snakes. I bet that if you put the photographs of these two species side by side, you would be unable to distinguish the poisonous one from the pet.8 Try it.
雄性孔雀鱼通过向雌性传递真实信号可能招来捕食者，而致命的珊瑚蛇却用鲜艳的体色吓退潜在天敌。这类拥有绚丽色彩与剧毒的蛇类约有九十种，原生于热带地区。它们身上红、黑与黄（或白）三色相间的环状花纹，在生物学上称为警戒色——这种显眼的色彩组合广泛存在于多种有毒动植物中，本质上是在向捕食者发出真实警告："敢碰我就死定了！" 但进化论若不够荒诞迷人，又怎称得上进化论？作为宠物饲养的无毒奶蛇，其体色与环纹几乎与珊瑚蛇如出一辙。我敢打赌，即便将两者的照片并列摆放，你也无法分辨孰毒孰宠——不信试试看。
14.
This kind of dishonest communication is called Batesian mimicry and was first discovered in the 1860s by Henry Walter Bates in butterflies of Brazil.9 In Batesian mimicry, a harmless species mimics the coloration of a poisonous or unpalatable species to ward off predators. Milk snakes have successfully evolved through natural selection to mimic coral snakes, thereby ensuring their survival.
这种欺诈性的交流方式被称为贝氏拟态，由亨利·沃尔特·贝茨于19世纪60年代在巴西蝴蝶中首次发现。在贝氏拟态中，无毒物种通过模仿有毒或难以下咽物种的体色来驱退捕食者。乳蛇通过自然选择成功进化出对珊瑚蛇的拟态，从而确保了自身生存。
15.
I want to present a necessary qualifier here, which I will assert a few more times to avoid anthropomorphic confusion. The words “honest” and “dishonest” in the discussion so far do not communicate the intent of the signaler. They point to the effect of the signal on the receiver. When a signal fools the receiver, it is dishonest; when the receiver understands the signal for what it is, it is honest. Neither the guppy nor the milk snake possesses the ability to dictate its body color. The orchid is not deliberately releasing pheromones, nor is the fiddler crab consciously building a large fake claw. These creatures are all products of natural selection honed over millions of years.
在此我需要提出一个必要的限定说明，为避免拟人化误解，这一点我会多次强调。前文讨论中使用的"诚实"与"欺骗"并非指信号发出者的主观意图，而是指信号对接收者产生的实际效果。当信号误导接收者时，即为欺骗性信号；当接收者正确理解信号本质时，则为诚实信号。无论是孔雀鱼还是乳蛇，都不具备自主控制体色的能力。兰花并非刻意释放信息素，招潮蟹也不是有意识地建造巨大的假螯。这些生物都是历经数百万年自然选择打磨的产物。
16.
For example, an ancestor of the harmless milk snake may have developed the coloration of the poisonous coral snake through a random mutation. These mutants would have had a higher survival rate than their nonmutated peers, thereby giving birth to more baby milk snakes with the same pattern. These babies, in turn, would have been safer from predators and would have successfully produced more offspring with the same colorful pattern, and so on. Over many generations, all milk snakes would come to have the same coloration as coral snakes. In doing so, they have perfected the dishonest signal. The Handicap Principle Clarifies It All While I call myself an investor, an evolutionary biologist would not be remiss in branding me a “signal decoder.” The only things investors can rely on to assess a company are the signals emitted by it—some direct and others indirect, some comprehensible and others bizarre, some ongoing and others delayed, and some quantitative and others qualitative.
例如，无毒乳蛇的祖先可能曾通过随机突变获得了有毒珊瑚蛇的体色。这些变异个体的存活率会高于普通乳蛇，从而繁衍出更多具有相同花纹的后代。这些后代又能更好地躲避天敌，进而成功孕育出更多携带相同艳丽花纹的子代，如此循环往复。经过无数代演化，所有乳蛇最终都进化出了与珊瑚蛇相同的警戒色，由此完美复刻了这种欺诈性信号。 "障碍原理"诠释一切 尽管我自称投资者，但进化生物学家将我称为"信号破译者"也恰如其分。投资者评估企业时唯一能依赖的，就是企业释放的各种信号——有些直接有些间接，有些明晰有些怪异，有些持续有些滞后，有些量化有些定性。
17.
Companies relay signals in the form of press releases, media interviews, analyst meetings, earnings calls, annual reports, dividends, buybacks, acquisitions, SEC filings, newspaper boasts, and so much more. Most investors receive the same signals from companies since the capital market regulators have ensured the standardization and transparency of communication. So how is it that the long-term performance of different investors—who are all within a small band of seemingly prodigious intellectual capacity—varies significantly? Among the many reasons for this variance, one that stands out for me is an investor’s ability to decode honest signals from dishonest ones (figure 7.1). But how is an investor supposed to distinguish between an honest signal and a dishonest one? Let’s turn to the Israeli evolutionary biologist Amotz Zahavi for an exquisitely elegant answer. But first, a brief background on signals. In the early and middle parts of the twentieth century, ethologists (ethology is the study of animal behavior) believed that signals were essentially honest indicators of senders’ underlying motives. However, in the 1970s, two British scientists, Richard Dawkins and John Krebs, took an almost diametrically opposite view claiming that signals were best seen as a deceitful manipulation by a sender to alter the behavior of the receiver for the sender’s benefit.10 On the other hand, the receiver was trying to fathom the true motive of the sender and plotting its own moves. According to Dawkins and Krebs, this leads to an arms race in which the sender and receiver are constantly trying to outdo each other.
但投资者该如何区分诚实信号与欺诈信号呢？让我们转向以色列进化生物学家阿莫茨·扎哈维，寻找一个精妙绝伦的答案。不过在阐述之前，需要先简要了解信号理论的背景。二十世纪早中期，动物行为学家们认为信号本质上是传递者内在动机的真实反映。然而到1970年代，英国科学家理查德·道金斯和约翰·克雷布斯提出了近乎截然相反的观点，他们认为信号应被视作传递者为谋取自身利益、意图操控接收者行为的欺骗手段。而接收者则试图洞悉传递者的真实动机并谋划对策。道金斯与克雷布斯指出，这将引发一场传递者与接收者不断试图压倒对方的"军备竞赛"。
19.
However, Amotz Zahavi rejected this pessimistic view of signals. In 1975, he proposed his famous “handicap principle,” which explains how and why honest signals can evolve in the natural world where animals are presumably trying to deceive one another.11 Zahavi’s profound observation was that when signals for a particular trait are costly to produce—he rightly termed such signals “handicaps”—and cannot be matched by another with a lower quality of that trait, they can be deemed “honest.” That may sound logical, but science, unlike the investment profession, needs empirical evidence to support its tall claims. The scientific community did not fully accept the handicap principle until fifteen years after the publication of the article in which Zahavi presented it.12 In 1990, two articles by the Scottish biologist Alan Grafen finally turned the tide in its favor.
然而阿莫茨·扎哈维驳斥了这种对信号传递的悲观解读。1975年，他提出了著名的"累赘原理"，阐释了在动物普遍存在欺骗行为的自然界中，诚实信号何以能够形成并持续演化。扎哈维的深刻洞见在于：当某种特质的信号需要付出高昂代价才能产生——他恰当地称这类信号为"累赘"——且该特质水平较低的个体无法效仿时，这类信号就可被视为"诚实"信号。这一理论看似合乎逻辑，但科学界与投资行业不同，必须用实证证据支撑重大主张。在扎哈维提出该理论的论文发表十五年后，科学界才完全接纳累赘原理。1990年，苏格兰生物学家艾伦·格拉芬发表的两篇论文最终扭转了学界态度。
20.
The best route to understanding the handicap principle is through carotenoids. Carotenoids are a class of pigments ranging from yellow to orange to red. They also provide health benefits by acting as antioxidants and strengthening the immune system. Animals can’t synthesize carotenoids within their bodies and must procure them by eating plants, bacteria, or fungi. Carotenoids play a strong signaling role, and scientists have studied their effect extensively in three species: the house finch (a bird), the three-spined stickleback (a fish), and the guppy. If the handicap principle is accurate, we should be able to confirm the following three hypotheses: 1. Females are differentially attracted to males with redder or brighter hues, 2. Males with a redder coloration are fitter than paler males, and 3. It is costly for healthy males to produce a deep red pigment.
理解"累赘理论"的最佳切入点是通过类胡萝卜素。这类色素呈现出从黄色到橙色再到红色的色彩谱系，不仅能作为抗氧化剂增强免疫系统，还具有促进健康的功效。动物无法自体合成类胡萝卜素，必须通过摄食植物、细菌或真菌来获取。类胡萝卜素具有显著的信号传递功能，科学家已在三种生物中深入研究了其作用机制：家朱雀（鸟类）、三刺鱼（鱼类）以及孔雀鱼。 若累赘理论成立，以下三项假设应能得到验证： 1. 雌性个体会显著偏好体色更红艳或更鲜艳的雄性， 2. 红色体色较浅色雄性更具生存优势， 3. 健康雄性个体需付出高昂代价才能生成深红色色素。
21.
In the species studied, researchers have shown conclusively that females react more favorably to males that display a deeper carotenoid pigmentation. Male house finches, for example, have three patches of carotenoid pigmentation on their body: on the crown, throat, and rump. Each patch varies in coloration from pale yellow to bright red. Experiments clearly show that female house finches prefer to consort with redder males whether this redness results from natural diet or artificial dyes. This finding supports the first hypothesis: receiver response.
在研究的物种中，研究者确凿地证明雌性对展现更深类胡萝卜素显色的雄性反应更积极。例如雄性家朱雀体表有三处类胡萝卜素显色区域：头冠、喉部和尾上覆羽。这些区域的色泽从浅黄到艳红不等。实验明确显示，雌性家朱雀更倾向于选择羽色更红的配偶——无论这种红色源自天然饮食还是人工染色。这一发现支持了第一个假说：接收者反应机制。
22.
To provide support for the second hypothesis (signal honesty), scientists demonstrated that well-fed house finches developed a deeper red coloration compared to those fed less nutritious food. When researchers exposed three-spined sticklebacks to parasites, the ones with a higher load of infectious ciliate had a significant decrease in red coloration. Other experiments like the one by Godin and McDonough described earlier have found evidence for the same phenomenon in guppies. Thus, deeper carotenoid pigmentation does seem to indicate better overall health.
为验证第二个假说（信号真实性），科学家证实营养充足的家朱雀比食物匮乏的同类能形成更深的红色羽毛。当研究人员让三刺鱼接触寄生虫时，感染纤毛虫数量较多的个体红色显著减退。戈丹与麦克唐纳早期的孔雀鱼实验等其他研究也发现了相同现象。这些证据表明，更深的类胡萝卜素色素沉积确实能反映更佳的整体健康状况。
23.
Let’s now deal with the last and most crucial hypothesis: whether honest signals impose a cost on the signaler. Over the years, biologists have unearthed four possible costs of deeper carotenoid pigmentation. First, carotenoid levels in fish scales or bird plumage are directly correlated with the amount of carotenoid in the animal’s gut. Thus, the more time an animal spends eating nutritious food, the redder the pigment but also the greater the cost incurred. Second, since carotenoids have health benefits and are needed to ward off disease and parasites, redirecting them from the gut to dead tissues like scales or feathers for display purposes is a costly maneuver for the animal. Third, displaying carotenoid pigmentation involves the cost of processing carotenoids in the gut and then transporting them to the feathers or scales. Hence, only those animals that have eaten enough nutritious food are up to the task. The fourth obvious cost is the risk of death: A deep red coloration charms females and foes alike. In laboratory experiments, trout have been shown to be more likely to attack redder sticklebacks, and blue acara cichlids are more likely to kill guppies with greater carotenoid pigmentation.
现在让我们探讨最后一个也是最关键的假设：诚实信号是否会让信号发出者付出代价。多年来，生物学家已发现类胡萝卜素着色加深可能带来四种代价。首先，鱼鳞或鸟类羽毛中的类胡萝卜素含量与动物肠道内的类胡萝卜素数量直接相关。因此，动物摄取营养食物的时间越长，色素就越红艳，但付出的代价也越大。其次，由于类胡萝卜素有益健康且能抵御疾病寄生虫，将其从肠道转移到鳞片或羽毛这类死组织中进行展示，对动物而言是种高昂的消耗行为。第三，展示类胡萝卜素着色需要付出在肠道中加工色素并将其运输到羽毛或鳞片的代谢成本，因此只有摄入充足营养的动物才能完成这一过程。第四种明显代价是死亡风险：鲜艳的红色既会吸引雌性，也会引来天敌。实验室研究表明，鳟鱼更易攻击颜色更红的棘鱼，而蓝色慈鲷更倾向于杀死类胡萝卜素含量更高的孔雀鱼。
24.
Thus, according to the handicap principle, redder male house finches attract more mates presumably because their message to females is, “Look how healthy and virile I am; I am so darned red!” It would be very costly and potentially even suicidal for a weak or diseased house finch to produce more carotenoid pigmentation given the diversion of resources required. Obviously, male house finches do not consciously “decide” to produce redder colors; the healthier ones simply have enough nutritional resources to produce the pigmentation, and the weaker ones don’t. Ditto for the brightly colored male guppy in the example mentioned earlier. Or the dude with the shiny red Ferrari. All of them show off ornaments that are very expensive to produce or procure and hence accurately communicate the signaler’s well-being. And herein lies Zahavi’s lesson for us investors: Lend credence only to those signals from companies that are costly to produce.
因此，根据累赘原理，羽毛更红的雄性家朱雀之所以能吸引更多配偶，很可能是因为它们向雌性传递了这样的信息："看我的羽毛多么红艳，这证明我无比健康强壮！"对于体弱或患病的家朱雀而言，若要将有限资源用于生成更多类胡萝卜素色素，不仅代价高昂，甚至可能危及生命。 显然，雄性家朱雀并非有意识地"决定"让自己变得更红——健康个体本就拥有足够的营养资源来生成色素，而虚弱个体则无法做到。前文提及的艳丽雄孔雀鱼，以及驾驶亮红色法拉利的炫酷男子，皆是如此。他们炫耀的这些装饰特征，要么需要耗费巨大成本来生成，要么需要付出高昂代价才能获取，因而能真实传递出信号发出者的优越状态。 这也正是扎哈维带给投资者的启示：只应重视那些企业需要付出高昂代价才能发出的信号。
25.
That, however, is easier said than done. What does “costly to produce” mean in the context of a corporate environment? What is the equivalent of a peacock’s resplendent tail? A shiny new corporate headquarters, an industry award, a growing market value, a CEO’s Forbes profile, or something else? I am a practicing investor, not a finance academic. I cannot offer a regression model proven to be statistically relevant, but I can offer the practical ways in which my team and I distinguish between honest and dishonest signals. Dishonest Signals Galore Let’s start with real-life examples of dishonest signals; that is, those that do not reliably communicate the trait they are supposed to. A “dishonest” signal does not imply that the sender is dishonest, although they might be; it simply means that the signal may not communicate what it is supposed to. Just as in the natural world, an honest person in a business setting can send a dishonest signal without necessarily having a bad intention.
然而，这说来容易做来难。在企业环境中，"高昂的生产成本"究竟意味着什么？孔雀华美尾羽的商业等价物是什么？是闪亮的新总部大楼、行业奖项、不断攀升的市值、CEO的《福布斯》专访，还是其他东西？作为实战派投资者而非金融学者，我无法提供经统计验证的回归模型，但可以分享我与团队在实践中区分真实信号与虚假信号的方法。 虚假信号泛滥 让我们先看几个现实中的虚假信号案例——即那些无法可靠传递其所宣称特质的信号。所谓"虚假"信号并不意味着发送者不诚实（尽管可能存在这种情况），仅说明该信号可能无法传达其本应传递的信息。正如自然界所示，商业环境中诚实的人也可能无意间发出虚假信号，而这未必出于恶意。
26.
When we evaluate a business to make an investment or divestment, we ignore the following types of signals or assign them a very low weight in our overall evaluation. Press Releases On July 29, 2014, Apple’s press release claimed that the company had updated the MacBook Pro with Retina Display (note the capital “R” and “D”).13 Should investors have cared about this news? Maybe you would have been impressed if you were a discerning consumer, but how would this announcement have helped an investor decide if Apple was a good company to buy? You could argue that the press release indicated Apple’s prowess in keeping up with the latest technology trends, and so it was, in fact, quite informative. But how many investors—or nontechies in general—even know or understand what “Retina Display” means? I don’t.
在评估企业以决定投资或撤资时，我们会忽略以下类型的信号，或在其整体评估中赋予极低的权重。 新闻稿 2014年7月29日，苹果公司发布新闻稿宣称已对配备Retina显示屏（注意"R"和"D"大写）的MacBook Pro进行更新。投资者真该在意这则消息吗？ 若作为挑剔的消费者，你或许会对此印象深刻。但这项声明如何帮助投资者判断苹果是否值得买入？你或许认为新闻稿体现了苹果紧跟技术潮流的能力，因此确实提供了有效信息。但有多少投资者——或者说非科技从业者——真正了解"Retina显示屏"的含义？反正我不懂。
27.
Even if Retina Display, whatever it is, were to turn out to be a cool new technology, how was anyone to conclude from this information that the MacBook Pro would become a success? And to belabor the point, even if an investor did understand what Retina Display meant, and even if they concluded from various sources that it was likely to be a great success, how would this knowledge help them decide whether to buy Apple shares unless they could accurately forecast the profits and cash flows to be made from the increased sales of the MacBook Pro as a result of this technology?
即便视网膜显示屏（无论它究竟是什么）最终被证明是一项酷炫的新技术，人们又怎能仅凭这一信息断定MacBook Pro将会大获成功？更进一步说，即便投资者确实理解视网膜显示屏的含义，即便他们通过多方信息推断这项技术很可能取得巨大成功——除非能准确预测该技术带动MacBook Pro销量增长所带来的利润与现金流，否则这类认知又如何帮助投资者判断是否该买入苹果股票？
28.
A press release is probably the most used—or should I say “abused”?—signal given out by companies. The reason? It does not cost anything for the company to be in the press regularly apart from the retainer for the PR agency, which is usually an annual fixed expense. Many companies resort to regular press releases and interviews to share news of product launches, management changes, strategy shifts, and organization changes. First, I am deeply suspicious of companies regularly in the press. (Don’t they have anything better to do?) But even those that are not professionals at managing the press through their public relations consultants rarely have anything helpful to say for a long-term investor. Second, press releases can be—and frequently are—more insidious than the harmless MacBook Pro announcement. Many press releases appear to reveal significant information that a long-term investor must absorb but turn out to be empty boasts at best and deliberate obfuscation at worst.
新闻稿可能是企业最常用——或许我该说"滥用"？——的对外信号。原因何在？对企业而言，除了支付给公关公司的固定年费外，定期发布新闻稿几乎无需额外成本。许多企业热衷于通过定期发布新闻稿和安排访谈，来宣布产品上市、管理层变动、战略调整和组织架构变更等信息。 首先，我对频繁亮相媒体的企业持高度怀疑态度（他们难道没有更重要的事可做吗？）。但即便是那些不擅长通过公关顾问操纵舆论的企业，也很少能为长期投资者提供有价值的信息。 其次，新闻稿可能——且往往确实——比无害的MacBook Pro新品发布通稿更具隐蔽危害性。许多新闻稿看似披露了长期投资者必须掌握的重要信息，实则充其量是空洞的自我吹嘘，往坏了说甚至是刻意混淆视听。
29.
Take the example of a Unilever press release from January 20, 2015.14 The headline states confidently, “Profitable Growth in Tougher Markets.” A detailed look at the press release left me scratching my confused head. I assume you agree that developed markets like the United States and Western Europe should be classified as “tough” because they are mature, have high competitive intensity, and have growth challenges. But if you read the press release carefully, you will notice that sales in 2014 declined by 0.8 percent in developed markets. Sales did grow 5.7 percent in emerging markets (though even this growth was lower than in 2013), but I would not call emerging markets “tough.”
以2015年1月20日联合利华的一篇新闻稿为例。标题信心十足地宣称："严峻市场中实现盈利增长"。细读新闻稿后，我却困惑地挠头不已。想必您会认同，像美国和西欧这样的发达市场应归类为"严峻"——因为这些市场已趋成熟、竞争激烈且增长乏力。但若仔细阅读该新闻稿，您会发现2014年发达市场销售额实际下降了0.8%。虽然新兴市场销售额确实增长了5.7%（尽管增速已低于2013年水平），但我认为将新兴市场称为"严峻"实在牵强。
30.
Interestingly, the press release fails to mention which markets, according to Unilever, are “tough” and which are “easy.” Maybe you disagree with me and prefer to call emerging markets tough. Alright, let’s go ahead with that definition. But then how does one explain a sales decline of 20 percent in the fourth quarter in China? A slowdown of this magnitude, especially in a market as large as China, may be relevant information for investors, but as we saw, it is nowhere in the headlines. Unilever’s overall sales in 2014, by the way, declined by 2.7 percent. Was this the cause for celebration that the press release seemed to imply? The bottom line is that making investment or divestment decisions based on a press release can be harmful to a fund’s bottom line. We treat press releases as what they are: a cheap signal given by companies to make competitors envious and to attract investors, employees, and customers. We don’t give them any importance. It was June 13, 2019. The fifty-one-year-old visionary CEO was giving an interview to Bloomberg’s Matt Miller on the sidelines of Europe’s most prestigious digital and tech conference in Berlin. His company was a giant in payment processing, and from being a scrappy start-up in the early 2000s, its soaring market value had enabled it to win an entry into the coveted DAX index in 2018. Its stock price had tripled over the past three years, valuing it at about €18 billion. Twenty-three of twenty-nine research analysts covering the company had a “buy” rating on it.
2019年6月13日，这位51岁富有远见的首席执行官在柏林欧洲顶级数字科技会议间隙，接受了彭博社记者马特·米勒的专访。他的公司已成为支付处理领域的巨头——从21世纪初一家不起眼的初创企业起步，凭借飙升的市值于2018年成功跻身令人艳羡的德国DAX指数。过去三年间公司股价翻了三倍，估值达到约180亿欧元。在覆盖该公司的29位研究分析师中，23位给予"买入"评级。
32.
Over the years, the Financial Times business newspaper and some short sellers had raised issues about its accounting practices, but the company had brushed aside all the allegations. In this context, Matt Miller was conducting an impressive interview. Rather than fawning over the celebrity CEO, Miller posed some tough questions on the sources of the company’s growth, its questionable corporate governance practices, and the reasons for the persistent rumors about the company’s lax compliance processes.
多年来，《金融时报》商业报纸和一些做空机构曾对其会计操作提出质疑，但该公司对所有指控均置之不理。在此背景下，马特·米勒进行了一场令人印象深刻的采访。他没有对这位明星CEO阿谀奉承，而是针对公司增长来源、存在争议的治理方式，以及为何持续出现其合规流程松懈的传闻，提出了一系列尖锐问题。
33.
The relaxed and confident CEO looked Miller directly in the eyes and answered every question with remarkable clarity and precision. 42.
Many publicly listed companies project their profits after tax (also called earnings) over the next fiscal year. For example, in January 2019, during their earnings call, they will project their earnings for the full year of 2019. In subsequent calls (usually held after every quarter), they update this guidance depending on their recent performance and outlook. Analysts and investors follow earnings guidance quite seriously, and stock prices react promptly to the under- or overachievement of this guidance. Investors and analysts love earnings guidance from companies. In fact, according to an article from PR Newswire, 77 percent of the 180 analysts surveyed wanted companies to provide guidance.16 Analysts are smart folks, so you must be thinking that they can learn a lot from the future pronouncements of companies. Sadly, they don’t.
许多上市公司会预测下一财年的税后利润（亦称盈利）。例如，2019年1月，公司在财报电话会议上会发布2019全年的盈利预测。在后续会议（通常每季度召开一次）中，公司会根据近期业绩与展望更新预测值。分析师与投资者高度关注盈利指引，股价也会因实际业绩低于或超出预测而迅速波动。 投资者和分析师对企业盈利指引青睐有加。事实上，据美通社文章显示，在接受调查的180位分析师中，77%希望企业提供盈利指引。分析师们才智过人，你或许认为他们能从企业的未来展望中获取大量信息。但遗憾的是，事实并非如此。
43.
What do you think a management team’s projections for the next year are based on? In fact, on what basis does anyone make forecasts about the near to medium term? The recent past? A hope for a better future? Spreadsheet manipulation? All of the above? Take your pick. Earnings guidance is a dishonest signal even though the management may fervently believe in it. It is cheap, easy to produce, and needs little effort to deliver. In my career spanning more than three decades, I have sat on the boards of more than two dozen companies ranging from a market value of $5 million to about $50 billion. At the beginning of every fiscal year, all these company management teams usually present their budgets for the year. And I don’t remember a single occasion when a management team hit its target. Let me repeat that: not a single occasion. I am not kidding. The businesses either exceeded their budgets or underperformed. And they had no way of knowing which would happen.
你认为管理团队对下一年的预测是基于什么制定的？事实上，任何人进行中短期预测的依据究竟是什么？是近期过往数据？对美好未来的期许？电子表格的数字游戏？还是以上全部？任君选择。盈利指引本质上是一种失真的信号，即便管理层对此深信不疑。它成本低廉、易于编制，几乎不费吹灰之力就能发布。 在我三十余年的职业生涯中，曾担任过二十多家公司的董事，这些公司市值从500万美元到500亿美元不等。每个财年伊始，所有公司的管理团队照例都会提交年度预算。而我记忆中从未有任何团队实现过既定目标——容我再次强调：一次都没有。绝非戏言。这些企业要么超额完成预算，要么未达预期，而他们事先根本无法预知结果。
44.
There is a simple reason why earnings are so hard to predict: multiplicative probability. Earnings—or profit after tax—is the last line item on a company profit-and-loss statement. We can calculate profit after tax after deducting from revenues the cash expenses, noncash expenses (like depreciation and amortization), financial charges, and taxes. Thus, the management needs to hit its target on all these line items to hope for an accurate earnings guidance. Many of these are not even under the management’s control. For example, interest rates may change, thereby affecting the interest income or expense. A global company selling in international markets can significantly undershoot or overshoot its budget owing to currency movements.
盈利预测之所以困难重重，根源在于概率的乘数效应。税后利润（即净利润）是企业损益表中的最后一行数据。从营业收入中依次扣除现金支出、非现金支出（如折旧与摊销）、财务费用及税款后，我们才能计算出净利润。因此，企业管理层必须确保上述所有分项指标均达成预期，才可能给出准确的盈利指引。 而其中诸多因素甚至不受管理层掌控。例如利率波动会影响利息收支；跨国企业的国际销售额可能因汇率波动而大幅偏离预算。
45.
But even the line items that you think are in management’s control aren’t usually so. Take revenues. Let’s say the management has confidently projected a certain level of sales for next year. But what if the economy grows faster than predicted? Or slower? What if the competitor launches a price war? What if one of the factories shuts down because of a fire? What if the company’s best sales manager, accounting for 7 percent of sales, falls ill suddenly? None of these “what ifs” is theoretical—they have all happened in businesses for which I have been a board member or investor.
但即便是那些你认为在管理层掌控范围内的项目，通常也并非如此。以收入为例，假设管理层信心十足地预测了明年的销售额。但如果经济增长超出预期呢？或是低于预期？如果竞争对手发起价格战呢？如果某家工厂因火灾停产呢？如果贡献7%销售额的王牌销售经理突然病倒呢？这些假设绝非空想——在我担任董事或投资人的企业中，它们都真实发生过。
46.
One may now assert that the costs incurred by a business should surely be under management’s control. After all, costs are all internal, aren’t they? Nope. Take raw materials. A manufacturing company using a wide variety of plastics and metals is subject to commodity price fluctuations; a logistics provider is held hostage to petroleum prices; and a services company needs to bend to the vagaries of labor market prices, especially if it is using part-time staff. Sales and advertising expenses seem more controllable—a marketing manager can decide to spend $10 million on advertising next year, and that’s that, right? Not so fast. If a new venture-backed competitor emerges that offers a similar product at a 25 percent discount, the marketing manager may have to react by increasing the marketing budget significantly.
如今人们或许会断言，企业产生的成本理应处于管理层掌控之中。毕竟所有成本都源于内部，不是吗？事实并非如此。以原材料为例：使用多种塑料和金属的制造企业会受大宗商品价格波动影响；物流服务商被石油价格所钳制；而劳务公司则需顺应劳动力市场价格变动——尤其是雇佣兼职员工时。销售与广告支出看似更可控，市场经理只需决定明年投入一千万美元广告预算即可万事大吉，对吗？别急。若突然出现一家获得风投的新竞争对手，以七五折价格提供同类产品，市场经理可能就不得不大幅增加营销预算来应对。
47.
Recall your basic probability class. You will remember that when many events are supposed to occur simultaneously (X and Y and Z), the cumulative probability is arrived at by multiplying all the individual probabilities. Let’s assume that the management you are evaluating is truly unique in its ability to forecast, and their predictive accuracy is 80 percent. Thus, the probability of hitting the right earnings number, if it is to be broken into just five component parts (revenues, discounts, employee expenses, manufacturing expenses, and sales expenses) would be less than 33 percent (0.8⁵)! In reality, the number of line items before we arrive at the earnings number is much more than five. So how would it be possible, even for a world-class management team, to keep hitting its earnings guidance numbers every quarter or every year? It is almost a mathematical impossibility. So what is the point of the financial community’s laser focus on this number?
回想一下概率基础课的内容。你会记得，当多个事件需要同时发生时（X与Y与Z），总概率是通过所有独立概率相乘得出的。假设你评估的管理团队确实拥有独特的预测能力，其预测准确率高达80%。那么，若将正确盈利数字拆解为五个组成部分（收入、折扣、员工成本、制造成本、销售成本），实现准确预测的概率将不足33%（0.8⁵）！现实中，构成盈利数字的细分项目远不止五个。那么即便是世界级的管理团队，又怎么可能每个季度或每年都精准达成盈利指引？这几乎是个数学上不可能完成的任务。既然如此，金融界为何还要执着地紧盯这个数字？
48.
I do not blame company management or analysts for this disease. Yes, I call it a disease because it infects almost everyone in the asset management industry and prevents investors from performing to their full potential. The blame for this malady should fall squarely on us, the investment community, which exerts enormous pressure on businesses and sell-side firms to come up with a single earnings number. A McKinsey analysis of earnings guidance reaches the following unflattering conclusion: “Our analysis of the perceived benefits of issuing frequent earnings guidance found no evidence that it affects valuation multiples, improves shareholder returns, or reduces share price volatility.”17 The beneficiaries of earnings guidance appear to be neither the investors nor the management—only the brokers! The reports concludes, “The only significant effect we observed is an increase in trading volumes when companies start issuing guidance.”
我并不将此弊病归咎于公司管理层或分析师。是的，我称之为弊病，因为它几乎侵蚀了资产管理行业的每个人，阻碍投资者发挥全部潜能。这种病症的责任应完全由我们投资界承担——正是我们对企业和卖方机构施加巨大压力，迫使它们给出单一的盈利数字。 麦肯锡关于盈利指引的分析得出了一个令人难堪的结论："我们对频繁发布盈利指引所谓优势的分析表明，没有证据显示它能影响估值倍数、提升股东回报或降低股价波动性。"17盈利指引的受益者似乎既非投资者也非管理层，而仅仅是经纪商！报告总结道："我们观察到的唯一显著影响，是当企业开始发布指引时交易量会上升。"
49.
And that’s not all. The sad irony of earnings guidance is this: A company consistently meeting its earnings guidance should be seen with an even greater degree of skepticism. Why? General Electric (GE). In the latter half of the twentieth century, GE was one of the most revered companies on the planet. And under Jack Welch, the CEO of GE from 1981 to 2001, it scaled heights never seen by any company on the planet.18 Under his leadership, the company increased its market value more than forty times over two decades. In August 2000, GE’s market value was $601 billion, making it the most valuable company on Earth. By the end of 2021, it was a shadow of its former self, having lost about 85 percent of its value.
不仅如此。盈利预测还存在一个可悲的反讽：对于总能达成盈利预测的公司，人们反而应该以更审慎的态度看待。 原因何在？看看通用电气（GE）便知。 二十世纪后半叶，通用电气曾是全球最受尊崇的企业之一。在1981至2001年担任CEO的杰克·韦尔奇带领下，这家公司达到了前所未有的巅峰。在他的领导下，通用电气市值在二十年间增长逾四十倍。2000年8月，其市值高达6010亿美元，成为全球最具价值的公司。而到2021年底，这家昔日巨头已黯然失色，市值缩水约85%。
50.
Many things went wrong at GE, and this is not the place to list them, but one of the most striking things about GE during the Jack Welch era was its ability to hit its earnings target quarter after quarter, year after year. Analysts and investors loved GE. When GE declared it would meet its earnings the next year, it always did. As Welch said in an interview with Carol Loomis of Fortune, “What investor would want to buy a conglomerate like GE unless its earnings were predictable?” The company had become so blasé and arrogant about “managing” its numbers that the CFO, Dennis Dammerman, was bold enough to admit to Fortune, “OK, we’re going to take these large gains and offset them with discretionary decisions with restructurings.” He was smoothening his profits in plain sight.
通用电气曾陷入多重困境，但此处不赘述其具体失误。杰克·韦尔奇执掌时期最令人惊叹的，是该公司总能季复一季、年复一年地精准达成盈利目标。分析师与投资者对通用电气青睐有加——只要它承诺次年实现预期收益，就从未食言。正如韦尔奇接受《财富》杂志卡罗尔·卢米斯采访时所言："若不能保证盈利稳定，哪个投资者会愿意收购通用电气这样的综合集团？"这家企业对于财务数据的"调控"已习以为常乃至傲慢自大，其财务总监丹尼斯·达默曼甚至公然向《财富》承认："没错，我们将通过重组等自主决策，用这些巨额收益来对冲调整。"他这是在众目睽睽之下平滑利润曲线。
51.
The managers were under enormous pressure to hit their earnings targets. When Jeff Immelt, the CEO after Jack Welch, took over the plastics division at GE and found that the previous management had been fudging the profits, he chose to keep quiet. He was a rising star within GE and knew that not meeting his budget was not an option. GE had a special-purpose vehicle called Edison Conduit that was supposed to be independent of the company but wasn’t. GE Capital guaranteed Edison’s liabilities, effectively making it a GE company. Why did it exist? Its stated purpose was to issue commercial paper, but its main objective was to create profits out of thin air. It periodically bought assets from GE Capital at higher prices than the book value, thereby showing profits. But how can a company buy its own assets to show profits, you ask? Good question. Today it can’t—it would be considered fraud. But the company was exploiting an accounting loophole of the pre-Enron era.
管理层承受着实现盈利目标的巨大压力。当杰克·韦尔奇的继任者杰夫·伊梅尔特接手通用电气塑料部门时，发现前任管理层一直在虚报利润，却选择了保持沉默。作为通用电气内部冉冉升起的新星，他深知预算目标必须达成。 通用电气曾设立了一个名为"爱迪生管道"的特殊目的实体，名义上独立于公司，实则不然。通用电气资本公司为该实体的债务提供担保，使其实质上仍属通用电气旗下。该实体为何存在？其宣称目的是发行商业票据，但主要目标实为凭空创造利润——它会定期以高于账面价值的价格从通用电气资本公司购入资产，从而制造盈利记录。你或许会问：企业怎能通过购买自家资产来虚增利润？问得好。如今这种行为已被视为欺诈，但当时公司钻了安然事件前会计制度的漏洞。
52.
In 2002, the Sarbanes–Oxley Act passed, and many accounting tricks being used by GE became illegal. Jack Welch had timed his retirement almost perfectly in 2001. Jeff Immelt, the new CEO, never got the leeway from the accountants that his boss had and kept missing investor expectations. GE’s stock price went into a decline that hasn’t been arrested more than two decades after Welch’s retirement. I am not suggesting that all of GE’s problems resulted from its guidance culture, but I think it played a significant role. When business managers are hyper-focused on meeting quarterly numbers, their long-term orientation takes a back seat; when a company is desperate to make an acquisition just to meet its earnings, it can overpay for a bad asset; when a manager is unable to speak the truth about accounting obfuscations, they pile up over time.
2002年《萨班斯-奥克斯利法案》颁布后，通用电气惯用的许多会计手段被列为非法。杰克·韦尔奇在2001年选择退休可谓时机精准，继任CEO杰夫·伊梅尔特再未能获得其前任享有的财务操作空间，屡屡辜负投资者预期。通用电气股价自此持续下跌，在韦尔奇退休二十余年后仍未止住颓势。 我并非将通用电气的所有问题归咎于其业绩指引文化，但认为这确是重要诱因。当管理者过度执着于达成季度指标，长远发展便退居次席；当企业为达成盈利目标而仓促收购，往往高价购入不良资产；当管理者对财务造假三缄其口，隐患便随时间不断累积。
53.
Last, let’s assume for a moment that a company can accurately predict its earnings without any accounting shenanigans of the kind employed by GE. So what? Should it matter to a long-term investor that the next year’s earnings will grow at 5 percent or decline by 10 percent? We base our assessment of a company on its riskiness, competitive moat, quality of financials, and management integrity—not on its ability to forecast earnings accurately. We know it is very hard to predict earnings consistently, so why measure anyone on it? We have never bothered to assess earnings guidance from companies and have tried to persuade many of our portfolio companies to cease providing guidance. The results, I will admit, have been mixed. Old habits die hard. Face-to-Face Meetings with Management
最后，我们姑且假设一家公司能够在不使用通用电气那种会计花招的情况下准确预测盈利。但这又怎样？对于长期投资者而言，来年盈利是增长5%还是下滑10%真的重要吗？ 我们评估一家公司的依据是其风险程度、竞争壁垒、财务质量和管理诚信——而非其精准预测盈利的能力。我们深知持续准确预测盈利极为困难，又何必以此苛责他人？我们从不费心研判企业提供的盈利指引，并曾试图说服投资组合中的多家公司停止发布这类预测。必须承认，成效参差不齐——积习难改。 与管理层面对面会谈
54.
In mid-2015, Valeant, a pharmaceutical company run by a McKinsey alum called Mike Pearson, was the darling of the stock market and had a market value of about $90 billion. Just five years before, in 2010, its market value was only about $5 billion. There was no business magazine that had not covered the stupendous success of Valeant. Nothing could stop this juggernaut acquiring pharma companies and cutting costs to the bone to enhance profits. Investors loved Mike and his seemingly unbeatable strategy, and marquee investors like Pershing Square, Sequoia, and ValueAct were big fans.
2015年年中，由麦肯锡校友迈克·皮尔森执掌的制药公司Valeant是股市宠儿，市值约900亿美元。而就在五年前的2010年，其市值仅为50亿美元左右。 没有哪家商业杂志不曾报道Valeant惊人的成功。这架战车通过收购药企和极致压缩成本来提升利润，势不可挡。投资者们钟爱迈克和他那看似无懈可击的策略，潘兴广场、红杉资本与ValueAct等知名投资机构都是其忠实拥趸。
55.
Charlie Munger, however, was having none of it. He rightly spotted that Valeant’s debt-fueled acquisition spree was a recipe for disaster. Combined with Valeant’s numerous unethical business practices (e.g., raising the prices of life-saving drugs many times after acquiring them), he opined that Valeant would fail spectacularly sooner or later. In a May 2016 interview with Fox Business, Munger called the company worse than a sewer!19 Munger’s views irritated some of Valeant’s largest shareholders, including Bill Ackman, the CIO of Pershing Square. He wrote a lengthy letter to Munger in April 2015 requesting him to meet with Mike Pearson, but Munger refused. Munger did not need a meeting to detect the “honest” signals of Valeant’s corruption and incompetence. Valeant had its comeuppance soon after and by mid-2016 had lost 90 percent of its value. Bill Ackman’s Pershing Square took a loss of $3 billion on Valeant.
然而，查理·芒格对此毫不买账。他敏锐地指出，瓦尔兰特公司依靠债务推动的收购狂潮注定酿成灾难。结合该公司诸多违背商业道德的行为（例如收购救命药后多次大幅提价），他断言瓦尔兰特迟早会惨烈崩盘。2016年5月接受福克斯商业频道采访时，芒格甚至斥责这家公司"比下水道还肮脏"！ 芒格的言论激怒了包括潘兴广场首席投资官比尔·阿克曼在内的多位大股东。2015年4月，阿克曼曾致长信恳请芒格与首席执行官迈克·皮尔森会面，但遭到拒绝。对芒格而言，根本无需会面就能洞悉瓦尔兰特腐败无能的"诚实"信号。该公司不久便遭报应——截至2016年年中，市值蒸发90%，致使比尔·阿克曼的潘兴广场蒙受30亿美元损失。
56.
Why were so many experienced investors blind to the problems that Munger had highlighted publicly? Why could Munger correctly predict Valeant’s demise without meeting Mike Pearson when many of Valeant’s board members and investors continued to be passionate supporters? My hypothesis is this: Mike Pearson was a fantastic salesperson. Correction. He was probably one of the best salespeople to have enamored Wall Street. The smartest people lost their sense of judgment when faced with Mike Pearson’s persuasive skills. Valeant’s honest signals of its myriad problems remained undetected or ignored in the light of the dishonest signals emitted by Mike Pearson in face-to-face meetings, which by definition were cheap to produce. Again, I am not asserting that Mike Pearson was dishonest. Maybe he genuinely believed what he said. It doesn’t really matter. The reason Munger was right was that he knew that any face-to-face meeting is a dishonest signal.
为何众多经验丰富的投资者对芒格公开指出的问题视而不见？为何芒格无需会见迈克·皮尔森就能准确预测凡利亚的衰败，而该公司董事会成员和投资者们却仍是其狂热支持者？我的假设是：迈克·皮尔森是个极擅推销的高手。确切地说，他或许是令华尔街为之倾倒的最强销售奇才之一。 当面对迈克·皮尔森的说服技艺时，最聪明的人也丧失了判断力。在皮尔森亲自出席的会议中，那些成本低廉却充满误导性的信号，掩盖了凡利亚公司真实存在的诸多问题——这些问题信号要么未被察觉，要么直接被忽视。 需要再次申明：我并非指控迈克·皮尔森存心欺诈。或许他真心相信自己所言。但这并不重要。芒格之所以正确，正是因为他深谙所有当面会谈本质上都是不可靠的信号。
57.
This claim that face-to-face meetings aren’t a reliable signal may seem strange at first glance given the importance of management teams for most investors, whether they are venture capitalists, private equity funds, or mutual funds. Many investors take pride in judging management teams based on a single meeting, and if you are one such investor, good for you. I am not. The odds are that many investors think they are good at judging people, but they generally aren’t. Let’s assume we encounter a genius who is genuinely able to judge a person’s character based on a face-to-face meeting. My assertion here is even if they possess this rare skill, it will be useless in a management meeting and will not enable them to become a better investor. The reason is simple: Any signal that management gives during a meeting is a cheap signal requiring minimal effort. And since it is cheap, the signal may be dishonest even if the management is not.
乍看之下，"面对面会议并非可靠信号"这一主张或许显得奇怪——毕竟管理团队对多数投资者（无论是风险投资人、私募基金还是共同基金）都至关重要。许多投资者以仅凭一次会面就能评判管理团队为傲，若你正是此类投资者，那很为你高兴。但我并非如此。 现实情况很可能是：多数投资者自以为擅长识人，实则不然。即便我们假设存在一位真正能通过面谈洞察人性的天才，我的核心论点在于：这种罕见能力在管理层会议中毫无用处，也无助于成为更优秀的投资者。原因很简单：管理层在会议中传递的任何信号都是低成本信号，几乎无需费力。正因其成本低廉，即便管理层本身诚实，这些信号仍可能失真。
58.
For example, management may believe that their product is high quality, and they will say so in a meeting. Would you believe their assertion, or would you do independent checks to assess the product’s quality? Management may genuinely believe that their recent underperformance relative to domestic competition is a result of their focus on overseas markets. If you are a good judge of people and conclude that management is telling the truth, would you take their stated belief to be true? Management knows what you want to hear, and quite often, that is what you will hear. We do meet management teams. We use these meetings to build relationships and to understand their corporate history and some of their past decisions. We never use management meetings to build an investment case or test key hypotheses because we know we will hear what they want us to hear, not what we want to know. Invoking Zahavi to Interpret Signals That Matter
例如，管理层可能认为自家产品质量上乘，并在会议中如此宣称。你会轻信他们的断言，还是通过独立核查来评估产品品质？管理层或许真心认为近期国内市场竞争失利源于其海外市场战略。即便你深谙识人之道并判定其言辞可信，是否就会全盘接受他们的观点？须知管理层清楚你想听什么，而你所闻往往正是他们想让你听到的内容。 我们确实会与管理层会面，但这类会谈仅用于建立关系、了解企业发展史及部分历史决策。我们从不依赖管理层会议来构建投资逻辑或验证关键假设——因为我们深知，会上只会听到对方想传递的信息，而非我们真正需要了解的真相。 援引扎哈维理论解析关键信号
59.
It looks like we are in a bit of soup here. If we can’t rely on press releases, investor conferences, earnings guidance, or management meetings, what kinds of signals can we, as investors, trust? Let’s go back to Amotz Zahavi, whose assertion about the natural world is highly relevant to the investing world: We should trust only costly signals emitted by companies because they are the only ones we can rely on. But what signals are costly to produce and hence reliable? Broadly, there are two. We know the first one from chapter 5. Past Operating and Financial Performance
看来我们现在的处境有些棘手。如果无法依赖新闻稿、投资者会议、盈利指引或管理层会议，那么我们作为投资者还能信任哪些信号呢？ 让我们回到阿莫茨·扎哈维的观点——他关于自然界的论断对投资界极具启发性：我们只应信任企业发出的高成本信号，因为唯有这些信号才值得信赖。但哪些信号因成本高昂而具备可靠性呢？主要有两类。 第一类信号我们在第五章已经讨论过： 过往经营与财务表现
60.
I will not rehash the logic I outlined in chapter 5 for visiting the past to assess a business and industry; suffice it to say that we at Nalanda treat the past as one of two critical and reliable signals for our investment and divestment decisions. The past has happened. It is there for us to see. It is an undeniable signal of the company’s actions and outcomes. The problem, of course, is that investors seem intently focused on receiving signals about the future. Let’s take the example of a ubiquitous phenomenon in the world of investing: conference calls.
我不会重复第五章阐述的逻辑——即通过回溯历史来评估企业和行业；只需说明一点：在那烂陀，我们将历史视作投资与撤资决策中两个关键可靠信号之一。历史已然发生，清晰可见，是企业行动与结果的无可辩驳的证明。问题在于，投资者似乎执着于接收关于未来的信号。 以投资界普遍存在的电话会议为例。
61.
I know I discussed conference calls in chapter 5, but it’s important to revisit the phenomenon in the light of honest and dishonest signals. A quarterly call is a strong signal sent by the management to the broader analyst and investor community. The CEO, CFO, and some other key business leaders typically attend the calls. If there is one place where you can get all your answers, this is it. Jamie Dimon of JPMorgan, Mark Zuckerberg of Facebook, Jack Dorsey formerly of Twitter, Tim Cook of Apple, Elon Musk of Tesla—all these business leaders regularly attend their companies’ quarterly calls.
虽然我在第五章讨论过电话会议，但结合诚实信号与虚假信号重新审视这一现象很有必要。季度电话会议是管理层向分析师和投资者群体发出的重要信号。首席执行官、财务总监及其他核心高管通常都会出席——若想一次性获得所有答案，这里就是最佳场合。摩根大通的杰米·戴蒙、脸书的马克·扎克伯格、推特前掌门杰克·多尔西、苹果的蒂姆·库克、特斯拉的埃隆·马斯克，这些商界领袖都定期出席各自公司的季度财报会。
62.
Let’s take a concrete example. Chevron, one of the world’s largest oil companies, had revenues of about $140 billion and a market value of about $190 billion in 2019. The management held a conference call for investors and analysts on January 31, 2020, to highlight its performance.20 The analysts and investors asked twenty-nine questions. What percentage of these do you think were about the future? More than 70 percent. Here is a small sample of these future-directed questions: • Can you talk a little bit more about how you think about volumes in 2020? (Remember that this call is from the start of 2020.) • I was curious where else we could see some upside in the portfolio in 2020. • As you continue to shed assets and capital intensity is lowered, just wondering how repeatable outsized dividend growth could be over the long term.
我们来看一个具体例子。2019年，全球最大石油企业之一的雪佛龙公司营收约1400亿美元，市值约1900亿美元。2020年1月31日，管理层为投资者和分析师举行业绩电话会议时，分析师与投资者共提出29个问题。这些提问中有多少比例涉及未来展望？超过70%。 以下是几个聚焦未来的典型提问： • 能否详细谈谈对2020年产量的预期？（请注意这是2020年初的会议） • 想了解2020年投资组合中还有哪些潜在增长点？ • 随着资产持续剥离和资本密集度降低，长期来看超额股息增长是否具备可持续性？
63.
In and of themselves, the questions on volumes, potential upside, and potential dividend growth are important. But just because a question is important does not mean it has a good or reliable answer. I want to know my fund returns next year, but I would be foolish to pick a number.
关于交易量、潜在上涨空间和股息增长潜力的问题本身固然重要。但问题重要并不意味着就能得到可靠答案。我想预知明年基金收益，但若妄下定论便是愚不可及。
64.
Let’s take the questions on volumes and potential upside. Chevron management gave a lengthy answer to these queries but essentially said that volume growth would be in the same ballpark as in previous years. Little did they—or anyone else for that matter—know that the COVID-19 pandemic would strike or that the price of and demand for oil would drop precipitously. Chevron would end 2020 with a net loss of $5.5 billion compared to a net profit of $2.9 billion in 2019. In the fourth quarter of 2020, revenue had dropped to $25 billion from $35 billion a year earlier, a reduction of almost 30 percent. During the conference call, management predicted capital spending of $21 billion in 2020. The actual number? $13 billion.

There is almost never a clear winner in the arms race between the senders and receivers of signals in the natural world. You may have heard of cuckoos leaving their eggs in the nests of other bird species so that someone else can rear their chicks. The signals here are the size, shape, and color of the cuckoo egg, which match those of the eggs of some other bird species. However, scientists have found that some of the parasitized bird species have not allowed themselves to be exploited—they have evolved to produce eggs that are distinctly different, even compared to eggs of their own species.24 As a result, they recognize cuckoo eggs as interlopers.
